• Apple Inc (NASDAQ:AAPL) tapped French premium television channel CANAL+ Group to broaden the reach of its Apple TV+ streaming service.

  • CANAL+ subscribers in mainland France can access Apple TV+ content under the multi-year deal.

  • The CANAL+ subscribers can view Apple TV+ content directly on myCANAL and in the CANAL+ universe of CANAL+ decoders, partners, and decoders.

  • Also Read: Real Life 'Ted Lasso'? Apple May Get Into English Football - Just Not AFC Richmond

  • CANAL+ says its subscribers cannot access Apple TV+ from the Apple TV app and will require a separate subscription.

  • The collaboration will help Apple reach a much wider audience. In France alone, CANAL+ has 9.5 million subscribers, TechCrunch reports.

  • CANAL+ has a presence in several European and African countries. It has more than 25 million paid subscribers in total.

  • In an interview with Variety, Apple's senior VP of services, Eddy Cue, said this is neither a temporary deal nor a promotion or a one-time thing. It's a many-years thing.

  • Price Action: AAPL shares traded lower by 0.43% at $164.84 on the last check Friday.
