Citigroup analyst Tyler Radke maintains Microsoft Corp (NASDAQ:MSFT) with a Buy and raises the price target from $282 to $332.




  • Amidst a prosperous catalyst path of generative AI monetization, the analyst sees a relatively uneventful Q3 set-up as macro and IT spending headwinds are more likely to negatively impact the numbers. 

  • Encouragingly, Radke's reseller checks and conversations trended more constructive this quarter, with better quota achievement and growth trends vs. Q2. 

  • Still, the analyst baked in incremental caution on Azure consumption with ongoing optimization headwinds, and his revised FY24 Azure growth estimates are 5 points below the Street. 

  • Even with lower numbers, the analyst sees an accelerating earnings growth story with expense growth moderation and top-line growth improving in Wednesday's note titled "Hitting Pause on ChatGPT to Look at FQ3 Earnings; Reseller Checks Better despite Obvious Near-term Headwinds."

  • Shares have recently re-rated vs. peers, but he believes there is more to go with MSFT trading at just ~30% premium to the S&P500 FCF yield despite 2-3x the top-line growth rates and a commanding position in GenAI.

  • B of A Securities analyst Brad Sills maintains Microsoft with a Buy and raises the price target from $300 to $320.



Price Action: MSFT shares were trading flat at $288.75 on the last check Wednesday.
