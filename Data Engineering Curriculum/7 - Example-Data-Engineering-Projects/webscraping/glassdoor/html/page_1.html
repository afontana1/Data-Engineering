              Pros

              Cons

It may be in a different division or directorate, but all the work is generally rewarding and important.

                  Great place for engineers of all career stages

                  Mechanical Engineer
                  Current Employee, more than 3 years
                  Livermore, CA
                  Recommend
                  CEO Approval
                  Business Outlook

                  Pros

                  - Stable, good pay, good benefits health. Retirement matching benefits are superior to industry competitors - Work life balance is great - Work culture is not toxic - Management is supportive of helping steer your role towards activities that interest you most - Plenty of opportunity to grow. You can take on responsibility at your own pace. That is, if you're ready to do more, management will put you in positions to be responsible for more. And if you want to progress more slowly while you craft other skills, management is also supportive of that. - Wealth of opportunities and different roles. You can really have a whole career where you try different jobs. Each engineering position has different roles that support the national security mission differently. If there is something new that interests you, there is opportunity to chase that. - Ability to have an tangible meaningful impact. At some places, engineers claim to have been involved with a project, when really they had a high level, minimal hands-on impact. At LLNL, you truly do get to be hands-on with projects you're working on.

                  Cons

                  - Pay is objectively great, but it is not on the level of high paying Silicon Valley companies. The work we do should be valued as highly as such companies considering the importance of the mission. - There are some bureaucratic processes that slow down progress on projects. On fast moving projects, rather than fixing the process, we suffer through it and let it stall progress to the point of missing deadlines.
